Mahama Coding School About Us

Mahama Coding School is a project led by Alight Rwanda in partnership with UNHCR and other partners to enhance the lifestyle of Refugee Youth by empowering them with the skills and knowledge in Sofware development by starting by Web development as the bridge for exploring other fields related to software development.

Gender-based Violance (GBV)

According to UNHCR, Gender-Based Violence is a serious violation of human rights and a life-threatening health and protection issue. When people flee their homes, they are often at greater risk of physical, sexual and psychological violence, such as rape, sexual abuse, trafficking and forced prostitution.

Although anyone: women, girls, men and boys; can fall victim to violence simply because of their sex or gender, women and girls are especially at risk. It is estimated that one in three women will experience sexual or physical violence in their lifetime.

Gender-based violence is preventable and UNHCR is committed to promoting gender equality and protecting displaced and stateless people from GBV.
