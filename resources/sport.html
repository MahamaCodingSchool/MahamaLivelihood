Sport and Entertainment

Indeed, the presence of various forms of entertainment in Mahama refugee camp holds significant importance. The cinema entertainment offered in the camp is particularly captivating, drawing the interest of both young individuals and adults. This form of entertainment not only serves as a source of enjoyment but also provides valuable opportunities for learning and guidance, especially as they receive advice and insights from their peers.

Fashion Entertainment

Initially, entertainment can be defined as the act of engaging in activities that offer amusement or enjoyment. In the case of Mahama refugee camp, entertainment was introduced in 2015 and has been an ongoing initiative ever since. This form of entertainment is accessible to individuals of all age groups, encompassing both the young and the elderly. The range of entertainment options available within Mahama camp includes cinema, theater, dance, music, and fashion.

Music

SPORTS IN MAHAMA REFUGEE CAMP

Mahama Refugee Camp, established in April 2015, has become a model for refugee management in the East African Region, accommodating over 50,000 refugees of various nationalities, with new arrivals joining regularly.

Sports, defined as activities that involve physical exertion, skill, and competition governed by formal organizations and rules, play a significant role in the well-being of refugees in Mahama camp. Various organizations such as UNHCR, Save the Children, and Plan International offer sports programs in the camp.

To promote the benefits of sports among refugees, the Mahama team organized a sports tournament featuring football, seated volleyball for individuals with disabilities, and basketball. The camp provides multiple basketball fields for playing and fostering talent.

basketball

An example of basketball being played in Mahama camp is illustrated by Daniella, a child participating in the sport. The sports activities organized by the companies bring together refugees and host communities, promoting harmony and minimizing conflicts.

Sports offer numerous benefits to refugees, including physical fitness and the opportunity for socialization. They teach important values such as hard work, teamwork, and competition. Football, being an international sport, captures the interest of many pupils in Mahama camp, contributing to the economic growth of nations and inspiring youth to develop their talents.

Volleyball

Another sport in the camp is sitting volleyball, specifically designed for players with disabilities. Participants are seated, creating an inclusive environment that aims to uplift children with disabilities, alleviate isolation, and foster happiness.
